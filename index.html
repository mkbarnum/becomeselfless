SELFLESS INC.

Supporting Efforts to Lead Families (and Individuals) to Life Long Education and Self-Sufficiency.

Programs

SELFLESS Begins With Self

We believe that the way to build stronger families and communities starts with individuals. Our mission is to empower people with the tools and resources to become self-sufficient. SELFLESS aims to offer these resources through our various financial need based programs. We focus on education and self improvement as the keys to self-sufficiency. SELFLESS really does begin with “self”.

FIND A PROGRAM FOR YOU

We encourage all program participants to be diligent in their efforts to improve their own lives so that they are able to better dedicate their time, talents, and energies to helping others in the future.

The College Assistance Program

The SELFLESS College Assistance Program (CAP) helps guide individuals to lifelong self-sufficiency by providing supplemental funds to attend a 4-year university as well as by providing individualized career coaching and training for financial literacy and life skills.

The EFY Assistance Program

The SELFLESS EFY Assistance Program promotes faith and leadership in youth by providing an opportunity for individuals – those who otherwise may not have the opportunity due to financial constraints –to attend Especially for Youth (EFY) summer camps.

University Campus Visit Program

The SELFLESS University Campus Visit Grant Program motivates youth to attend college by providing an opportunity to visit university campuses in preparation for attending a university.

Missionary Assistance Program

The SELFLESS Missionary Assistance Program provides an opportunity for individuals – those who otherwise may not have to opportunity due to financial constraints – to increase their faith as well as gain leadership, management, and other valuable skills through serving a full-time mission for the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints.

Job Improvement Program

The SELFLESS Job Improvement Program promotes self-sufficiency and aid to individuals and families by providing financial training and career coaching, as well as helping meet the basic needs of the recipients, for short periods of time, so that they can focus their efforts on improving technical skills and long-term job acquisition.
