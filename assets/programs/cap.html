THE COLLEGE ASSISTANCE PROGRAM

APPLY NOW

PURPOSE

The purpose of the College Assistance Program (CAP) is to help guide individuals to lifelong self-sufficiency by providing supplemental funds to attend a 4-year university as well as by providing individualized career coaching and training for financial literacy and life skills. Participants will work with qualified professionals to create and follow a budget, as well as receive additional career counseling and coaching as needed. A coach will help determine the amount of funds needed, to supplement any earnings or savings the recipient has, to pay for costs such as: tuition, fees, books, supplies, room, board, travel, and transportation expenses.

GENERAL GUIDELINES

The SELFLESS College Assistance Program (CAP) is a unique opportunity for individuals seeking to improve their lives and become self-sufficient by attending a 4-year university.

This is not a traditional scholarship program. SELFLESS does not award predetermined amounts as part of the CAP program. If you are selected as a recipient, you will meet with a coach to review your finances and determine the amount to be awarded each semester based on your personal needs. Recipients will be required to apply for FAFSA (fafsa.gov) and are encouraged to work a part-time job while attending school. As a recipient, you will also have the opportunity to receive coaching and counseling from qualified professionals about financial literacy, career choices, and other personal matters as needed. This program is designed to enable recipients to graduate from school without debt, improve their future career potential, and have the financial management knowledge necessary to be self-sufficient throughout their life.

The SELFLESS is to help as many families and individuals as possible. To accomplish this, we strongly encourage recipients to attend Brigham Young University, either in Provo, UT or Rexburg, ID. Both schools offer an excellent education at a reasonable price (BYU is listed #6 for Best Value Schools: U.S. Universities). If you do not wish to attend one of these schools, recipients may also enroll in BYU Pathway or the BYU Flex program for a year to gain Utah residency prior to transferring to another school in Utah, such as Utah Valley University or Utah State University. If you have already been accepted to a school or have plans to attend a different school, please specify this in your application. Your choice of school will be taken into consideration when evaluating your application. Exceptions to the above guidelines may be made on a case-by-case basis. If you think that your situation merits an exception, please explain this in your 2nd essay (Self-Sufficiency Essay) and it will be considered.

CRITERIA & ELIGIBILITY

  1. Applicants must have graduated high school, or be on track to graduate.
  2. Applicants must demonstrate community involvement, improvement in academics, and a desire to attend college.

CONDITIONS TO MAINTAIN

  1. Participants must attend a 4-year university or college, or be enrolled in BYU Flex or BYU Pathway as a part of a plan to subsequently enroll in a 4-year university or college.
  2. Participants must maintain a 3.0 GPA. Each semester recipients must submit an unofficial transcript to be reviewed.
  3. Participants must complete and submit a budget of all estimated living costs each semester/quarter. The budget will be reviewed to determine the qualified grant amount.
  4. Participants must enroll in a minimum of 14 credit hours per semester/quarter.
  5. Participants must apply for Federal Student Aid through FAFSA (fafsa.gov) each year.
  6. Participants are also encouraged to maintain a part-time job working 8-10 hours a week.
  7. Participants must comply with the conditions above. If these conditions are violated, SELFLESS has the right to revoke the grant or allow an additional semester/quarter as a probationary period. If the probationary period is granted and the recipient fails again to meet the requirements, the grant may be revoked.
